# Water and Ratios

A cup of coffee is more than 98% water. If your tap water tastes bad on its own, your coffee will taste bad too.

![Pouring water from a kettle](../assets/03-water-and-ratios.png)

## Water that works

- Use filtered water if your tap water is heavily chlorinated or very hard.
- Avoid distilled water — coffee needs some mineral content to extract well.
- Brew between 195°F and 205°F (90–96°C). Off the boil is fine for most methods.

## A ratio you can remember

Start with **1 gram of coffee for every 16 grams of water** (a 1:16 ratio).

- For a single mug, that is roughly 18 g of coffee to 290 g of water.
- For a full press, scale up: 36 g of coffee to 580 g of water.

A small kitchen scale is the single biggest upgrade most home brewers can make. Eyeballing scoops introduces more variance than any other step.

## Dialing it in

- Coffee tastes flat or watery? Use slightly more coffee, or grind a bit finer.
- Coffee tastes harsh or astringent? Use slightly less coffee, or grind a bit coarser.
