# Brewing Methods

Three methods cover almost every home setup. Each one highlights different qualities in the same beans.

![A pour-over and a French press side by side](../assets/04-brewing-methods.png)

## Pour over

Clean, bright, and great for showing off a single-origin coffee.

1. Heat water to just off the boil.
2. Rinse the paper filter, then add a medium grind.
3. Pour 2× the coffee weight in water and let it bloom for 30 seconds.
4. Pour the rest in slow circles to hit your target weight.
5. Total brew time: 3 to 4 minutes.

## French press

Fuller body, more texture, very forgiving.

1. Add a coarse grind to the press and pour all the water in at once.
2. Stir gently and put the lid on without pressing.
3. Wait 4 minutes, then press slowly and pour right away.

## Espresso

Concentrated, intense, and the base for milk drinks. Espresso needs a dedicated machine and a fine, consistent grind. If your shots run too fast, grind finer; too slow, grind coarser. Aim for roughly a 1:2 dose-to-yield ratio in 25 to 30 seconds.

## Choosing one

If you only pick one method to master, start with the pour over. It teaches you to control every variable in this course at once.
