# Your First Cake: Classic Vanilla Sponge

This is the cake to learn first. It uses the creaming method, only six ingredients, and works in a single 9-inch round pan.

## Ingredients

- 225 g (1 cup) unsalted butter, softened
- 225 g (1 cup plus 2 tablespoons) granulated sugar
- 4 large eggs, at room temperature
- 225 g (1 ¾ cups) all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 2 tablespoons whole milk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- A pinch of salt

## Method

1. **Heat the oven** to 350 °F (175 °C). Line a 9-inch round pan with parchment and butter the sides.
2. **Cream the butter and sugar** on medium-high until pale and fluffy, about 4 minutes.
3. **Add the eggs one at a time**, beating well after each. If the mixture looks curdled, add a spoonful of the measured flour.
4. **Whisk the flour, baking powder, and salt** together in a separate bowl.
5. **Fold in the dry ingredients** in two additions, then stir in the milk and vanilla until the batter is just smooth.
6. **Bake for 30 to 35 minutes**, until the top is golden and a skewer comes out clean.
7. **Cool in the pan for 10 minutes**, then turn out onto a rack to cool completely before slicing or frosting.

## Common fixes

- **Sunken middle.** Oven was opened too early, or the batter was overmixed.
- **Dry crumb.** Baked too long, or too much flour. Weigh, do not scoop.
- **Dense texture.** Butter and sugar were not creamed long enough, or eggs went in too cold.
