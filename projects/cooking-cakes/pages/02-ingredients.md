# Essential Ingredients

Almost every cake recipe is a variation on the same six ingredients. Once you know what each one does, you can read any recipe and predict how the cake will turn out.

## The core six

- **Flour** — builds structure. All-purpose flour is the default; cake flour gives a softer crumb.
- **Sugar** — sweetens, but also tenderizes and helps the cake brown.
- **Eggs** — bind the batter, add richness, and help it rise.
- **Fat (butter or oil)** — adds flavor and moisture. Butter gives flavor; oil gives a moister cake.
- **Leavening (baking powder or baking soda)** — makes the cake rise.
- **Liquid (milk, buttermilk, or water)** — hydrates the flour and dissolves the sugar.

## Why ratios matter

A cake is a balance. Too much flour and it turns dry. Too much sugar and it sinks in the middle. Stick to the recipe by weight, not by volume, until you understand how the ingredients behave.
