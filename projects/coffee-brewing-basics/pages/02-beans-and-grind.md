# Beans and Grind

Great coffee starts before any water touches the grounds. The two variables that matter most are bean freshness and grind size.

![Coffee beans and a grinder](../assets/02-beans-and-grind.png)

## Pick fresh beans

- Look for a **roast date** on the bag, not a "best by" date. Aim for beans roasted within the last two to four weeks.
- Buy whole beans. Pre-ground coffee loses most of its aroma within a few days.
- Store beans in an airtight container away from heat and sunlight.

## Match grind to method

The right grind size is the one that lets water flow through the bed at the right speed for your brewer.

- **Coarse** — French press, cold brew.
- **Medium** — drip machines, pour over with a flat-bottom dripper.
- **Fine** — espresso, Moka pot.

If your coffee tastes weak and sour, try a finer grind. If it tastes harsh and bitter, try coarser. Adjust one variable at a time.
