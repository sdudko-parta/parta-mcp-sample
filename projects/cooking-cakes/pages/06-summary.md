# Summary

You now have the foundation to bake a cake from scratch and understand why each step matters.

## What you covered

- **Ingredients.** Flour builds structure, sugar adds tenderness and color, eggs bind and enrich, fat carries flavor and softens the crumb, and leaveners give the rise.
- **Tools.** A reliable oven thermometer, a kitchen scale, and a sturdy pan matter more than any specialty gadget.
- **Techniques.** Creaming aerates butter and sugar, folding preserves that air, and gentle mixing keeps the crumb tender.
- **A first recipe.** A classic vanilla sponge that comes together with six core ingredients and the creaming method.

## What to try next

- Bake the vanilla sponge twice — once by weight, once by volume — and compare the crumb.
- Swap vanilla for citrus zest or almond extract to learn how flavorings carry.
- Try a two-layer version and practice leveling, filling, and a simple buttercream.

## Remember

Baking rewards measurement and patience. Weigh your ingredients, bring them to room temperature, and trust the method before you start improvising.
