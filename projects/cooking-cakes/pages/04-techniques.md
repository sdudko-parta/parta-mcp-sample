# Core Techniques

A cake batter is mostly about how you combine things, not what you combine. Three techniques cover almost everything you will encounter.

## Creaming

Beat softened butter and sugar together until the mixture is pale and fluffy. This takes three to five minutes with a mixer. You are pushing air into the fat — that air is what makes the cake rise and gives it a tender crumb. Under-creaming is the most common reason a butter cake comes out dense.

## Folding

Combine a light mixture (like whipped egg whites or whipped cream) with a heavier one (like batter) without deflating it. Use a flexible spatula. Cut down through the middle, sweep along the bottom, and turn the mixture up and over. Rotate the bowl a quarter turn and repeat. Stop the moment you no longer see streaks.

## The muddle in the middle: don't overmix

Once flour meets liquid, gluten starts to form. A little gluten gives structure; too much gives you bread. Mix only until the flour disappears. A few small lumps are fine.
