# Tasting and Improving

The fastest way to brew better coffee is to taste your current coffee with intent.

![A tasting flight of three small cups](../assets/05-tasting-and-improving.png)

## A simple tasting routine

1. Let the coffee cool for two or three minutes. Hot coffee hides flavors.
2. Take a small sip and let it cover your whole tongue.
3. Ask three questions: Is it sweet? Is it sour? Is it bitter?
4. Write down a one-line note. "Lemony, thin" or "Heavy, bitter finish" is enough.

## What the flavors tell you

- **Sour and weak** — under-extracted. Grind finer or use a longer brew time.
- **Bitter and harsh** — over-extracted. Grind coarser or shorten the brew.
- **Flat and dull** — usually stale beans or bad water. Fix the inputs first.

## Building a feedback loop

- Change one variable at a time. Otherwise you cannot tell what helped.
- Keep a running note in your phone with your dose, ratio, grind setting, and tasting notes.
- After a week of small adjustments you will have a recipe tuned to your palate, your beans, and your gear.

That feedback loop is the whole game. Equipment helps, but attention is what makes coffee great.
